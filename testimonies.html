GLADDENS

Student

Growing up, I struggled with school due to a learning disability that went undiagnosed until age five, leaving me feeling inadequate and ashamed of my academic struggles. However, when my family relocated to Canada for a more inclusive education things began to change. In this new environment, I discovered diversity and received support tailored to my learning needs, which allowed me to embrace my uniqueness and let go of the pressure to conform to arbitrary standards. With the encouragement of supportive teachers, I learned to appreciate my strengths, particularly as a kinesthetic learner, leading to my success in rhythmic gymnastics, culminating in participation in world championships. Reflecting on my journey, I now recognize that past failures were instrumental in discovering my true capabilities, and I credit my parents' sacrifices for enabling my growth and achievements. Happy FAIL-A-BRATION!
