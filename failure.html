Encouraging Failure

Introduction

CPS, or Creative Problem Solving is a way for designers to find a better and greater solution to a problem. When it comes to solving a problem, there are many things that should be considered. The CPS is basically a guideline for designers on how to start from brainstorming to ideate and finally implementing the solution.

When it comes to problems, we can categorize them. There are two categories of problems which are wicked and systematic.

  1. Wicked Problem:

    2. wicked problem is a complicated problem that cannot be solved with one answer. It cannot be solved only by one solution as there are many factors that follow with the problem which makes it difficult to solve. Designers who use the model are to use the CPS as a method to break down the problem, and untangle the problem to find the best out of different solutions that come up. e.g) global warming, climate change, world hunger, affording housing.

  2. Systemic problem:

    3. Systemic problem is the problem connected with the surroundings that we have in daily life. For example, Thailand had improved the health system, and based on the improvement, they were able to make a bigger change in the overall economy. The systemic problems are like chains connected to one another, as one problem may factor from another by another. By breaking down the problem, and finding the categorizing it into systems, designers can find a bigger picture and find a pathway to tackle problems that will solve problems.

How Do We Encourage Failures?

Based upon human observations, the CPS is Creative Problem Solving, which Is a method of solving a problem. The method was first developed by Alex Osborn.

He has worked on the creative process based on his work field. He worked on the process and developed it with his partner Sydney Parnes. Later on, many other authors have developed their own based on Osborn's.

The first CPS had seven steps, but since then, many authors have refined the model, and now it is down to 4 main categories.

The Model that uses the 4 steps is called the FourSight Model. The categories of the model starts from Clarify, Ideate, Develop, and Implement.

  1. Clarify:

    2. clarifying is the stage of understanding the problem. Inspecting the problem, designers are to explore the problem they have found, and gather the data that. With good understanding of the problem and gathering enough data, designers then are to look for ideas that will help solve the problem. It is essential that designers fully understand the problem, and then gather data. The data can be collected through interviews, observations, ethnography, or traditional research.

  2. Ideate:

    3. In the ideate stage, Designers are to brainstorm ideas based on the problem they have untangled/clarified. It is recommended for the designers to follow divergent and convergent thinking which is the thought process that will help to find a better idea for the problem. In Diverge, designers are to bring up any ideas possible that may be a good solution to the problem. It is recommended to bring up as many ideas as possible at this stage. Then, in the Cognition thinking process, designers are recommended to filter out ideas based on logical thinking and questions asking to oneself whether the idea is useful in real life or not.

  3. Develop:

    4. in this stage, designers are to develop the idea they have based on what they have from the previous stage. There are different activities designers may use to develop ideas such as worst idea possible where designers can bring up all kinds of ideas that would be the worst solution to be for the problem, Brain Writing where designers are to bring up good ideas and develop another after the previous, and Analogy/Mashup Method where designers may look at other methods and solutions some companies may previously have thought of.

  4. Implement:

    5. It is the final stage of CPS method, and where designers are to be put in action to what they have as a solution for the problem. However, if there are problems to be found to the solution, it is recommended for the designers to go back to the stages of the CPS method and get a better solution for it.
